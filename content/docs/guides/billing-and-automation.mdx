# Plans, Billing, and Alerts

This guide helps you understand what happens when you hit plan limits, when payment fails, and when TrustLoop is trying to reach the right people during an active incident.

## Plan limits

TrustLoop tracks usage against your workspace plan.

### Included daily allowances

| Plan | Incidents | AI triage runs | Customer updates | Reminder emails |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Starter | 50 | 100 | 100 | 120 |
| Pro | 200 | 300 | 300 | 500 |
| Enterprise | 1,000,000 | 1,000,000 | 1,000,000 | 1,000,000 |

If your team depends heavily on AI triage, customer-update drafting, or automated reminders, review this page before you hit those limits during a busy incident period.

## What happens when payment fails

TrustLoop does not immediately cut the workspace off.

### Expected payment-failure flow

1. Billing moves into a past-due state.
2. Workspace owners and managers receive payment reminder emails.
3. If payment is still not fixed after 48 hours, TrustLoop downgrades the workspace to Starter.

This means you should treat a payment-failure email as an operational issue, not just an accounting note.

## How reminder alerts work

TrustLoop can continue nudging the team while an incident stays open.

### Standard reminder behavior

- Open incidents can trigger reminder emails to the owner.
- The exact reminder timing depends on workspace settings.
- Reminders stop once the incident is resolved.

### `P1` escalation behavior

For urgent incidents, TrustLoop can go beyond email.

It may use:

- The assigned owner
- Managers or owners in the workspace
- On-call rotation settings when enabled
- Browser push notifications
- SMS when phone numbers are available

## What to check when alerts do not fire

### No reminder email arrived

Check:

- The incident still has an owner
- The incident is still open
- The workspace has not exhausted reminder-email allowance

### No SMS arrived for a `P1`

Check:

- The owner or on-call responders have phone numbers saved
- The workspace is configured for urgent escalation

### No push notification arrived

Check:

- Push notifications were enabled in the browser
- Notification permission was granted
- The browser is still subscribed for your account

## Billing page checklist

Open **Settings -> Billing** when you need to:

- Upgrade or change plans
- Apply a coupon code
- Confirm the current billing status
- Review whether a payment failure was detected

<Callout type="info" title="Know your operational limits before you need them">
  The safest time to review reminder behavior, plan allowances, and escalation coverage is before a real incident. A short internal drill makes billing and alert gaps visible while the stakes are low.
</Callout>
