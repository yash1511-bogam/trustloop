# Sign In and Access

TrustLoop supports multiple authentication methods. Use the method configured by your organization or workspace administrator.

## Authentication Methods

### Email Verification Code

A one-time verification code is sent to your registered email address. Recommended for:

- Initial account access
- Passwordless authentication
- Session recovery

### Google or GitHub OAuth

Authenticate using your existing Google or GitHub identity. Recommended for:

- Streamlined sign-in
- New workspace creation
- Workspaces where your email matches an existing user record

### SAML SSO

Authenticate through your organization's identity provider. Recommended for:

- Enterprise workspaces with centralized identity management
- Organizations enforcing access policies through an IdP

### Team Invite Links

Workspace owners and managers can send invite links via email. Invite links route you to the correct workspace and associate your identity with the pending invitation.

## Common Workflows

### Creating a New Workspace

Navigate to **Register** and authenticate using one of the following:

- Google OAuth
- GitHub OAuth
- SAML SSO (if pre-configured)
- Email verification code

### Signing In to an Existing Workspace

Navigate to **Login** and authenticate using the same method used during registration.

### Accepting a Team Invite

Open the invite link first. TrustLoop will route you to the appropriate registration or sign-in flow for the target workspace.

### Recovering Access

Navigate to **Forgot Access** and request a recovery code using your registered email address.

## Post-Authentication Behavior

- TrustLoop matches your identity to an existing workspace account by email or identity provider.
- If the workspace slug is missing, it is populated automatically during the authentication flow.
- After successful authentication, you are redirected to the dashboard.

## Troubleshooting

### OAuth redirects back to the sign-in page

Retry the sign-in from the same page where you initiated the flow. TrustLoop expects the OAuth callback to return to the originating page before completing the redirect.

If the issue persists, verify whether your account exists. You may need to use **Register** instead of **Login**.

### "No workspace account" error after OAuth

Your email was authenticated, but no matching workspace user was found.

- Use **Register** to create a new workspace.
- Contact a workspace owner to receive an invite if you are joining an existing workspace.

### Email verification code not received

- Check spam and junk folders.
- Confirm you are using the email address associated with your workspace.
- Verify whether your organization requires SAML SSO instead of email-based authentication.

<Callout type="info" title="Authentication email delivery">
  Verification codes for sign-in, registration, and recovery are sent by the authentication service. If codes are not arriving, investigate email delivery and identity configuration rather than workspace or billing settings.
</Callout>

### SAML returns "invite required"

Your identity was validated, but your workspace account has not been provisioned. Request an invite from a workspace owner or manager.

### Invite link is invalid or expired

Contact the workspace owner or manager to revoke the expired invite and issue a new one.

### Incorrect workspace opens after sign-in

Authenticate using the email or identity method associated with the target workspace. For SAML-enabled workspaces, initiate sign-in from the workspace slug provided by your administrator.

## Administrator Recommendations

- Maintain at least two owners per workspace for continuity.
- Use individual invites rather than shared email accounts.
- For SAML workspaces, verify the workspace slug and IdP metadata configuration before onboarding users at scale.
